Conversation Without Common Sense (CWCS)

Conversation Without Common Sense takes a humorous approach toward improving the experience in human-chatbot interaction through testing where the equilibrium lies between the machine feeling unfriendly and them being distinctively inhuman through disambiguating the hidden process beneath the chatbot responding to your messege, animating the interface to emphasize their attentive characteristics, and creating a space for more by eliminating gender and race.

Prototype no.1

The first prototype experimented with how, in an augmented reality environment, a chatbot can help bringing the molecular particles in our lives up to a scale that we can be friends with, by making up the identical characteristics of the chemical element's behavior. The conversation would be accompanied by the cartoony icons of the characters moving around to give the illustion of attention. The demonstration can be found here.

Prototype no.2

On the second stage, the targeted question is to find the frequency of blinking on the moving face icon that feels the most friendly to average users. The perception test is designed and posted to receive public results. The test can be found here. It composts of three stages including:
  1. Introduction and greeting interface which informs the tested subjects
  2. Control group: a 15-second animation of the symbol appearing, blinking every 3 seconds, and disappearing
  3. Test group: a 15-second animation of the symbol appearing, blinking (randomly chosen from) for never/ every 7 seconds/ every 2 seconds/ only when at the edge of the display area, and disappearing
